Question 1

Can you tell us about your journey as an artist and how you discovered that happiness would be the central theme of your work?

My artistic journey began with a fascination for color and expression, but it took me some time to find my true voice. I wanted to create something that felt meaningful, not just to me, but to those who would view my work. Happiness emerged as a recurring theme because I believe it’s a universal feeling everyone seeks, yet we often overlook it in our busy lives. Art became my way of reminding people to pause and reflect on the moments of joy that already exist around them.

Question 2

How do you balance the personal and universal aspects of happiness in your artwork?

I draw a lot from my personal experiences, but I aim to express emotions in a way that resonates with a broader audience. Everyone’s journey to happiness is different, but certain feelings—like the warmth of the sun on your face or the excitement of new possibilities—are things most people can relate to. I think the balance comes naturally when I stay true to my emotions, but I also keep in mind the viewer’s perspective.

Question 3

In today’s world, where there is so much stress and uncertainty, how do you think your art contributes to people’s sense of well-being?

I think the role of art is crucial, especially in times of stress or hardship. My hope is that my art offers a brief escape, a reminder that happiness can still be found, even in small moments. The colors and themes I use are meant to uplift and soothe the viewer, offering a counterbalance to the negativity or anxiety they might be feeling. Art can act as a refuge, a space where people can reconnect with their sense of wonder and joy.

Question 4

Where do you find inspiration for your pieces centered on happiness?

Inspiration comes from many places, but most often from nature, music, and everyday interactions. I love observing the natural world—there’s so much beauty in something as simple as a flower blooming or sunlight filtering through trees. Music also plays a huge role in shaping my mood and ideas. I’m inspired by the joy in small, often overlooked moments, like sharing a meal with friends or feeling the breeze on a summer day.

Question 5

What message do you hope people take away from your art?

I hope people walk away from my art with a lighter heart. If they can leave with a renewed appreciation for the small joys in life, or if my work helps them feel more connected to their own happiness, then I feel like I’ve succeeded. Ultimately, I want my art to be a gentle reminder that happiness is within reach—it just takes a moment to notice it.
